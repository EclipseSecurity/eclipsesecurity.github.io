About Eclipse Security

Eclipse Security is a collective of eight college friends united by a shared passion for security research. Our interests span offensive and defensive security, malware analysis, artificial intelligence, and capture-the-flag (CTF) challenges, enabling us to approach security from multiple technical perspectives.


Our members have released a range of projects, including vulnerable driver research such as GhostKatz and GoodBaiii, ETW and detection-focused research like COMmander and EventHorizon, and Inline-EA. While these projects were released individually, they were strengthened through collaboration, peer feedback, and shared expertise within the group.


We founded Eclipse Security to unify our research under a single identity. Although we have long supported one another through platforms such as LinkedIn and Twitter, bringing our work together in one place allows for greater visibility and impact across the broader security community.

Mission

Our goal is simple: to produce meaningful research, support each other's growth as security practitioners, and contribute valuable tools, knowledge, and insights back to the community.
